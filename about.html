The NariVibes Story

Our Story

NariVibes — French for 'The Summer' — was founded by Nari, a textile designer from Chennai who spent years studying natural dyeing in Lyon before returning home with a singular vision: to create garments that feel like a second skin in the summer heat. She wanted to reconcile the European sensibility for quiet luxury with the extraordinary hand-skills of Indian weavers.

What began as a small studio in Auroville, crafting silk slips from fabric remnants, grew into a collection that now ships to women across India and beyond. Every piece is made in small batches to eliminate overproduction, and every design is guided by a philosophy of effortless permanence — clothes made to be worn for decades, not seasons.

"I wanted to make something that felt like the air after rain."

— Nari, Founder

Values

What We Believe In

hourglass_top

Slow Fashion

We design twice a year, in small batches. No fast drops, no endless SKUs. Every collection is considered, numbered, and intentional — born from months of research into craft, colour, and the way women actually live in their clothes. We are at peace with fewer, better things.

visibility

Radical Transparency

We publish the full cost breakdown of every garment — materials, labour, overhead — because we believe you deserve to know what you are paying for. Transparency is not a marketing strategy for us; it is the foundation of the trust we are trying to build, one honest garment at a time.

architecture

Indian Craft Heritage

From block-printed linens to hand-loomed silks, our techniques trace back centuries. We are custodians, not just creators — working with artisan families to document, preserve, and evolve skills that deserve to live far beyond our generation. Every piece is a collaboration between past and present.

Master weavers of handloom cotton, working on pit looms that date back generations. The Maheshwari weave — known for its distinctive border and reversible fabric — is a UNESCO-recognised craft. We have worked with this community since 2020.
